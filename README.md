# Football and the FIFA World Cup 2026


## What Is Football?

Football, also known as soccer in some countries, is a team sport played by two teams of 11 players each. The main objective is to score more goals than the opposing team within 90 minutes of normal time, divided into two halves of 45 minutes.

Some basic football rules:

- Each team has 10 outfield players and 1 goalkeeper.
- Players may not touch the ball with their hands or arms, except for the goalkeeper inside their own penalty area.
- A goal is scored when the whole ball fully crosses the goal line.
- Fouls can lead to free kicks, yellow cards, or red cards.
- If a knockout match ends in a draw, it may continue to extra time and a penalty shootout.

## About the FIFA World Cup 2026

The FIFA World Cup 2026 is the 23rd edition of the FIFA World Cup. This tournament is historic because, for the first time, the World Cup is hosted by three countries:

- Canada
- Mexico
- United States

The 2026 World Cup is also the first edition to use a 48-team format, expanded from the previous 32-team format.

## Tournament Format

The 2026 World Cup uses a larger format than previous editions:

- Total participants: 48 national teams.
- Number of groups: 12 groups.
- Teams per group: 4 teams.
- Each team plays 3 matches in the group stage.
- The top two teams from each group advance to the knockout stage.
- The eight best third-placed teams also advance.
- The knockout stage begins with the Round of 32.

With this format, more countries have the opportunity to compete on the World Cup stage.

## Why the 2026 World Cup Matters

The 2026 World Cup is important for several reasons:

- It is the first World Cup with 48 teams.
- It is the first World Cup hosted by three countries.
- The number of matches increases, giving more nations a chance to participate.
- The tournament expands football's reach, especially across North America.

## Things to Watch

When following the 2026 World Cup, some interesting things to watch include:

- The performance of the host nations: Canada, Mexico, and the United States.
- Title favorites such as Argentina, Brazil, France, Spain, England, or Germany.
- Debutant teams or smaller nations that may create surprises.
- Star players performing on the world stage.
- Team strategies in a longer group and knockout format.

## Important Terms

| Term | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Group stage | The opening stage where teams play within their groups |
| Knockout stage | The elimination stage; the losing team is eliminated |
| Clean sheet | A match where a team does not concede a goal |
| Hat-trick | A player scores three goals in one match |
| Extra time | Additional time played after a draw in the knockout stage |
| Penalty shootout | Penalty kicks used to decide the winner |
| VAR | Video Assistant Referee, technology used to help match officials |

## Conclusion

The FIFA World Cup 2026 is one of the largest editions in football history. With 48 teams, 16 host cities, and a new format, the tournament gives more nations around the world the chance to compete at the highest level.
